Academic Structure

Developing sharp minds with a foundation of reasoning, research, and responsibility

Our curriculum is designed to provide a comprehensive and engaging learning experience for students from Class 1 to Class 12. Each class has a unique set of subjects and activities that cater to the developmental needs and interests of the students. Below is an overview of the curriculum for each class, including core subjects, research projects, and extracurricular activities.
  • Understanding the difference between hardware and software
  • Learn basics of C and C++ through playful examples (40 small programs annually)
  • Explore HTML and simple webpage creation
  • Play-based UI/UX learning (e.g., arranging blocks, screen design)
  • Story-based intro to Git and version control ("Saving your work")
  • Inspire creativity through app/web design thinking
  • Simple robotics intro using toys
  • Types of sensors in planes, boats, robots (via videos)
  • Watch NPTEL lectures (simplified for kids)
  • Draw your own invention ideas
  • Counting (1–100), skip counting, number sense
  • Shapes, patterns, puzzles to stimulate logic
  • Place value, building numbers with blocks
  • Addition & subtraction with objects and games
  • Clock reading, days, months (story calendar)
  • Money recognition with real coins
  • Length, weight, volume with measurement games
  • Pictographs & bar charts using stickers and visuals
  • Solid, liquid, gas exploration (ice melting, boiling water, air in balloons)
  • Color mixing & reactions (baking soda + vinegar)
  • Everyday chemistry: soap, salt, sugar, water
  • Explore light, sound, and motion through activities
  • Simple experiments like rolling objects, shadows, magnet play
  • Observe how things move and why (gravity, friction)
  • Ask questions like "Why does the ball stop?"
  • Human body parts & their functions
  • What plants need to grow (light, water, soil)
  • Animals and their babies
  • Environmental care: reduce, reuse, recycle
  • Story reading, vocabulary building, picture writing
  • Foundation of grammar through stories
  • Daily speaking activities, show & tell
  • GRE/TOEFL base exposure via creative English games
  • What is right and wrong?
  • Simple introduction to rules and justice
  • Classroom rules as base of legal understanding
  • My family tree and local history
  • Important Indian figures in simple stories
  • How people lived in old times (past vs. present)
  • Needs vs. wants
  • Simple idea of trade (using toys, snacks)
  • Understanding money, saving, spending (piggy bank)
  • Our Earth and sky
  • Basic landforms: mountains, rivers, oceans
  • Weather and seasons
  • Map of India and local area
  • Understanding rules and fairness through stories
  • Respect for diversity, basic rights and responsibilities
  • Role of leaders, classroom elections
  • Barakhadi, simple words and rhymes
  • Picture stories and vocabulary
  • Spoken Hindi games and moral stories
  • Start with Hindi and English foundation
  • Basic introduction to any regional or 3rd language
  • Stories, songs, rhymes for language growth

  2. Saying sorry and thank you
  3. Clean hands, clean heart
  4. Sharing with friends
  5. Kindness to animals
  6. Listening to elders
  7. Helping in small ways
  8. Truthfulness in daily life
  9. Respecting everyone
  10. Taking turns and waiting
  11. What makes you happy?
  12. Year-End Reflection
  • Famous people and places
  • Inventions and discoveries (kid-friendly)
  • Festivals, flags, national symbols
  • Motor skill games: running, jumping, hopping
  • Yoga and balance poses
  • Team games and sportsmanship
  • Team activities and group games
  • Learning together: puzzles, LEGO, art
  • Understanding differences and helping peers
