    The Financial Need Index (FNI) is a score (1‚Äì100) assigned to every student based on financial background and family circumstances. It helps determine support eligibility.

  • How is FNI Calculated?
    • Annual Family Income (40 points): Lower income = lower score; higher income = higher score.
    • Family Structure (20 points): Orphaned/single-parent score lower.
    • Assets & Property (15 points): Ownership boosts score.
    • Financial Burden (10 points): Chronic medical issues or debt lower score.
    • External Aid (5 points): Social/government support modifies score.
    • Educational Expenses (10 points): If unable to afford supplies or books, score remains low.
