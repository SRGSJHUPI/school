AMBEDKARITE VALUES

  • Education First: We believe education is the path to liberation. Our faculty prioritize learning, reason, and knowledge above rituals or dogmas.
  • Self-Respect and Dignity: No student should feel inferior. We cultivate confidence, reject discrimination, and build dignity through dialogue and discipline.
  • Equality Without Compromise: Whether the barrier is caste, religion, gender, or class—our educators teach students to question, reject, and rise above all forms of inequality.
  • Critical Spirituality: We do not promote blind faith. Faculty encourage respectful dialogue across faiths, empowering students to challenge injustice in any tradition.
  • Reject Slavery of Any Kind: Emotional, religious, or social manipulation has no place in our school. If anything demands your rights in exchange for loyalty, leave it behind. Liberty is non-negotiable.
  • Fraternity and Social Responsibility: Inspired by Buddha’s Sangha and Ambedkar’s movement, we promote collective progress, team learning, and mutual care among students.
  • Voice Against Injustice: Silence in the face of wrong is betrayal. Our faculty teach students to speak, write, and act against injustice—peacefully but firmly.
  • Scientific Temper and Rational Thinking: Decisions must be guided by reason, evidence, and logic—not superstition or authority. We foster scientific inquiry across subjects.
  • Lifelong Learning and Evolution: No one is “fully educated.” Our faculty constantly learn, unlearn, and update their own thinking to stay accountable to students and society.

💡 Financial Need Index (FNI) Policy

  • What is FNI?

    The Financial Need Index (FNI) is a score (1–100) assigned to every student based on financial background and family circumstances. It helps determine support eligibility.

    • 1: Extreme financial hardship (orphaned, no support).
    • 100: Wealthy, can afford premium education (₹10+ lakhs/year).
  • How is FNI Calculated?
    • Annual Family Income (40 points): Lower income = lower score; higher income = higher score.
    • Family Structure (20 points): Orphaned/single-parent score lower.
    • Assets & Property (15 points): Ownership boosts score.
    • Financial Burden (10 points): Chronic medical issues or debt lower score.
    • External Aid (5 points): Social/government support modifies score.
    • Educational Expenses (10 points): If unable to afford supplies or books, score remains low.
    • FNI Score Interpretation:
    FNI Range Interpretation Support Eligibility
    1 – 20 Extreme hardship, highest need Full scholarships & support
    21 – 40 High need, major constraints Major discounts, aid for books, materials etc.
    41 – 60 Moderate need Partial scholarships & discounts
    61 – 80 Minor need, stable finances Limited aid (books/ uniforms)
    81 – 100 Financially self-sufficient No aid eligibility
  • Documentation Required: Income certificates, tax returns, asset documents, medical certificates, and any other relevant proofs to verify the FNI score.
  • Confidentiality: All personal information is handled with strict confidentiality, and used only for support decisions.
  • Review & Appeals: Families may request score review if their financial status changes, with new documents subject to verification.

👩‍🏫 TEACHER SELECTION POLICY AT RISING NALANDA - TWO TYPES

📈 1. TEACHER PROFIT-SHARING & ENGAGEMENT POLICY

  1. Objective

    To build a committed team of educators who share the long-term vision of Rising Nalanda, this policy establishes a performance-linked profit-sharing arrangement in place of a fixed salary. Teachers will be valued partners in growth, with defined rewards and responsibilities.

  2. Nature of Association
    • Teachers will be engaged as Academic Partners under a contractual agreement for this school only.
    • This does not create any legal ownership, directorship, or shareholding in the school But teacher joined under this policy can not be fired from school untill majority of teacher under this policy agrees or he/she become threat for school existance.
    • Academic Partners will be entitled to a defined percentage of profit or equivalent compensation as detailed below.
  3. Profit-Sharing
    • Each teacher shall be entitled to:
      • Either: TPS - Teacher Profit Share (before tax)
      • Or: ₹2,00,000 (Two Lakh Rupees) per month. Whichever is lesser.
    • TPS will Vary for a particular teacher and will be determined based on TSI

      • Teacher Strength Index (TSI) will be calculated based on the following parameters:

      • Experience and Qualification Factor (EQF): decided by Director.
      • School Management Factor (SMF): contribution to curriculum development, management or public reputation of the school, decided by Principal and Director.
      • Length of Service Factor (LSF): = 1 + (Current Year - TJY)/200, where TJY = Teacher Joined Year (e.g., 2028).
      • Teacher Joined Factor (TJF): = (3000 - TJY)/1000.
      • Feedback Factor (FF): depends on student results, feedback, and involvement in school growth.
      • Contribution to innovation,
      • Other Miscellaneous Factors (OMF): decided by school management via anonymous voting.
      • TSI Calculation: TSI = EQF × SMF × LSF × TJF × FF × OMF
      • Although TSI will be calculated by AI (Artificial Intelligence), the Director has the final authority to adjust the TSI.
      • All factors are liable to change depending on conditions

      Teacher Profit Share(TPS):
      TPS = [(30% of school's net profit) / TNT] × TSI
      Note: TNT = Total Number of Teachers (including both Core Partner and Regular Contract Teachers).

  4. Vesting / Eligibility Conditions
    • Must understand Ambedkarite values
    • 50% of the eligible amount will vest after the first year, and 100% after three years of continuous service.
    • Leaving the school before completion of a vesting period will lead to forfeiture of unvested benefits.
    • New Teacher under this policy will be allowed after approval of teachers already under this policy but ultimate authority will be in hands of director.
  5. Role and Decision Framework
    • Although all teachers shall have the right to advise and recommend on academic and operational matters but Teachers under this policy will have a kind of authority.
    • The final decision-making authority rests solely with the School Founder / Management.
    • Teachers are expected to uphold the school’s ethical, academic, and disciplinary standards.
  6. Security Deposit / Retention Clause
    • A fixed percentage (e.g., 10–15% of monthly profit share or equivalent) shall be retained as Security Deposit.
    • The Security Deposit will be refundable upon voluntary and approved exit with proper notice (minimum 3 months).
    • If a teacher exits without approval or violates the contract, the Security Deposit will be forfeited.
    • Either party may terminate the agreement with three months’ written notice.
    • On termination, only vested profit-share shall be payable.
    • Any unvested portion or security deposit may be forfeited for early exit, misconduct, or violation of policy.
  7. Dispute Resolution
    • Any disagreement arising from this policy shall be resolved amicably through discussion.
    • If unresolved, the matter shall be referred to a mutually appointed arbitrator, and the decision of the Founder shall be final.
  8. Acknowledgement
    • By signing this agreement, the teacher confirms that they:
      • Understand and accept the terms of this policy
      • Acknowledge that this is not an employment relationship but a profit-linked partnership
      • Agree to work in alignment with Rising Nalanda’s vision and mission

📝 2. REGULAR CONTRACT TEACHERS POLICY

  1. Nature of Employment

    Regular Contract Teachers are engaged on a fixed-term contractual basis with Rising Nalanda. This arrangement does not constitute permanent employment and is subject to annual renewal based on performance and conduct.

  2. Salary and Benefits
    • Teachers will receive a monthly salary as mentioned in their appointment letter, payable at the end of each month.
    • Increment or revision of salary will depend on:
      • Teaching performance
      • Student feedback
      • Discipline and contribution to the school
      • School’s financial condition
    • Teachers may receive additional incentives, bonuses, or rewards for outstanding results or extra work, at management’s discretion.
  3. Duties and Responsibilities
    • Must understand Ambedkarite values
    • Deliver lessons as per the timetable, curriculum, and school standards.
    • Prepare lesson plans, assignments, and tests on time.
    • Maintain student records, attendance, and academic progress.
    • Participate in school functions, workshops, training sessions, and extracurricular activities.
    • Support students’ holistic development, discipline, and values.
    • Maintain classrooms and learning environments in a clean, respectful, and motivating condition.
  4. Code of Ethics
    • Uphold Rising Nalanda’s vision of ethical, scientific, and inclusive education.
    • Maintain honesty, respect, and care in all professional interactions.
    • Promote a positive learning environment that reflects the values of the institution.
  5. Dispute Resolution
    • In case of any disagreement, the matter shall be discussed internally with the management.
    • If unresolved, the Founder’s decision will be final and binding.
  6. Declaration
    • By signing the appointment letter, the teacher acknowledges that they have read, understood, and agreed to follow all rules and regulations mentioned above.
  • Note: The school reserves the right to modify or update these policies as needed, with prior notice to all teachers.
  • Important: Teachers can apply for both policies but can only be selected for one policy at a time.
  • Any Teacher once selected for any of two policies cannot switch it against school's approval. Although they can request for change.

* These policies are effective from January 1, 2026, and supersede any previous versions.
