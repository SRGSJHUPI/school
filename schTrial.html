📘 Curriculum Overview (Class 1)

🎯 Goal: Build curiosity about how computers work, and foster logical thinking through playful code exposure.
  • Understanding hardware and software.
  • Basics of C and C++ through 40 beginner-level problems.
  • Code explanation by teachers; no pressure to code alone.
  • HTML basics and introduction to web development.
  • Basic UI/UX and Git concepts via stories and visuals.
🎯 Goal: Develop strong number sense, logical reasoning, and pattern recognition.
  • Counting (1–100), skip counting.
  • Shapes and patterns exploration.
  • Place value understanding using blocks.
  • Addition & subtraction with two-digit numbers.
  • Time: reading clocks, days and months.
  • Money: coins and bills recognition.
  • Measurement: length, weight, volume.
  • Pictographs and simple data interpretation.
🎯 Goal: Stimulate scientific observation and curiosity about motion, force, and light.
  • Motion: rolling balls, ramps, slides.
  • Light and shadows with torches/sunlight.
  • Sound: identifying sounds and their sources.
  • Magnet experiments and friction exploration.
🎯 Goal: Explore basic properties of materials and simple reactions.
  • Solids, liquids, gases (ice melting, water boiling).
  • Mixing colors and substances.
  • Everyday chemistry: soap, salt, water, sugar.
  • Baking soda + vinegar volcano experiment.
🎯 Goal: Learn about the human body, plants, animals, and environment.
  • Body parts and basic functions.
  • Types of animals and their habitats.
  • Plant growth experiment (seeds in cotton).
  • Environmental care: don't litter, reuse, water plants.
🎯 Goal: Build language, imagination, and early comprehension.
  • Story reading and retelling.
  • Phonics and vocabulary games.
  • Basic grammar through storytelling.
  • GRE/TOEFL early exposure via vocabulary cards.
🎯 Goal: Understand basic rules, roles, and fairness in society.
  • What is a rule? Why do we follow them?
  • Classroom democracy: mock voting.
  • Respecting all people and opinions.
🎯 Goal: Differentiate needs/wants and understand simple exchange.
  • Needs vs wants activity.
  • Roleplay shop and barter system.
  • Saving in a piggy bank.
🎯 Goal: Develop historical thinking and awareness of past events.
  • Personal timeline: birth to now.
  • Family stories from grandparents.
  • Basic festivals and historical places in India.
🎯 Goal: Build awareness of surroundings, Earth, and environment.
  • Landforms: hills, rivers, plains.
  • Seasons and weather patterns.
  • Simple map of India and local area.
🎯 Goal: Understand right vs wrong through simple examples.
  • What are laws? Why are they important?
  • Fairness and justice in school and life.
  • Respecting others' rights and property.
🎯 Goal: Develop strong language foundation using poems and stories.
  • Barakhadi and simple matras.
  • Hindi rhymes and short stories.
  • Picture-based vocabulary games.
🎯 Goal: Build empathy, truthfulness, respect, and kindness.
  • Clean hands, clean heart.
  • Helping and sharing.
  • Being truthful and patient.
  • Kindness to animals.
  • Respecting elders.
🎯 Goal: Strengthen listening, reading, and speaking in multiple languages.
  • Basic vocabulary in Hindi and English.
  • Exposure to 3rd language via rhymes/stories.
  • Daily reading and speaking routines.
🎯 Goal: Build teamwork, social behavior, and shared learning.
  • Group puzzles and team building games.
  • Collaborative art and storytelling.
  • Understanding each other’s strengths.
🎯 Goal: Develop motor skills, fitness, and sportsmanship.
  • Running, hopping, skipping activities.
  • Ball games and yoga stretches.
  • Outdoor play and group sports.
🎯 Goal: Broaden knowledge of world, country, and nature.
  • Important people, places, inventions.
  • Animals, birds, countries, flags.
  • Daily current events (age-appropriate).
🎯 Goal: Spark innovation and early research interest.
  • Explore sensors in devices (plane, boat, robot).
  • Intro to robotics and ML via NPTEL (simplified).
  • Mini-projects with team presentations.
🎯 Goal: Strengthen logical understanding and code tracing ability through guided practice.
  • Reinforce basics of C, C++, and logic-building exercises.
  • 50+ small problems with increasing difficulty.
  • Introduction to HTML tags and simple page building.
  • Use of Git for tracking classroom projects.
  • UI/UX with interactive design blocks.
🎯 Goal: Improve problem-solving and visual number sense using applied arithmetic.
  • 2-digit addition and subtraction (word problems).
  • Skip counting in 2s, 3s, 5s, 10s.
  • Basics of multiplication using groups and arrays.
  • Length, weight, time: applied measurement tasks.
  • Data: bar graphs and comparison charts.
🎯 Goal: Deepen observation-based learning about light, force, and machines.
  • Friction and smooth surfaces comparison.
  • Simple machines: wheel, lever, pulley (toys).
  • Shadow and mirror experiments.
  • Sound vibrations using rubber bands, bowls, spoons.
🎯 Goal: Recognize changes in states and properties of common substances.
  • Solubility: sugar, salt, sand in water.
  • Mixtures and separating methods (filtering, sieving).
  • States of matter activity: freeze, melt, boil.
🎯 Goal: Observe and relate human, plant, and animal life to surroundings.
  • External organs and their functions.
  • Types of plants, parts, and uses.
  • Food chains and habitats (with art activities).
🎯 Goal: Strengthen grammar and sentence making through stories and activities.
  • Reading level-2 storybooks.
  • Parts of speech basics (noun, verb, adjective).
  • Speaking: describing pictures and objects.
  • Vocabulary enrichment via games and flashcards.
🎯 Goal: Understand leadership, responsibility, and community roles.
  • Leaders around us: teacher, head boy/girl, etc.
  • Mock classroom parliament activity.
  • Respect for different cultures and voices.
🎯 Goal: Basic financial literacy and value of things.
  • Roleplay: buyer & seller (price tags, fake currency).
  • Budgeting games: what to buy with ₹100.
  • Needs vs luxury item sorting.
🎯 Goal: Understand personal and local history through visuals and stories.
  • Family trees and timelines.
  • Stories of Indian kings and freedom fighters.
  • Historical monuments with drawing activity.
🎯 Goal: Develop spatial thinking and understand Earth’s basic layout.
  • Continents and oceans (with globe).
  • Climate zones and dressing accordingly.
  • River, hill, desert, plains: drawing and recognition.
🎯 Goal: Learn fairness, equality, and why rules apply to everyone.
  • Rights of a child (UN convention simplified).
  • Breaking vs. following rules activity.
  • Honesty in school and at home.
🎯 Goal: Build sentence structure and story writing in Hindi.
  • Short stories and creative writing.
  • Grammar basics (ling, vachan, kriya).
  • Picture-based sentence formation.
🎯 Goal: Deepen understanding of responsibility, honesty, and kindness.
  • Helping at home and school.
  • Respect for others' opinions.
  • Being thankful and forgiving.
🎯 Goal: Build fluency in listening, reading, and speaking in 2+ languages.
  • Daily reading sessions in Hindi and English.
  • Basic third language introduction: numbers, greetings.
  • Story retelling in multiple languages.
🎯 Goal: Improve collaboration and empathy through group work.
  • Group presentations, class discussions.
  • Team-building puzzle solving.
  • Rotating leadership in tasks.
🎯 Goal: Boost physical fitness, coordination, and sports habits.
  • Jumping, skipping, balancing tasks.
  • Stretching, yoga poses.
  • Intro to team games (relay, tag, etc.).
🎯 Goal: Encourage curiosity about the world and surroundings.
  • Important Indian personalities and inventions.
  • Simple science and nature facts.
  • National symbols, festivals, and world flags.
🎯 Goal: Build inquiry-based thinking and creativity through mini-projects.
  • Observation journal: "How does it work?"
  • Build a sensor-powered toy (teacher-guided).
  • Watch & reflect on robotics videos.
🎯 Goal: Strengthen programming logic and introduce real-world computing.
  • Deeper understanding of C/C++ syntax and logic tracing (20–30 new problems).
  • Use of loops, conditionals, and input/output functions.
  • HTML topics: headings, links, lists, tables, images, and forms.
  • UI/UX design basics (spacing, color, navigation) with drag-and-drop tools.
  • Version control: Git basics, commits, and saving student projects.
  • Introduction to Scratch programming (used in U.S. primary curriculum).
🎯 Goal: Advance arithmetic thinking, logical puzzles, and visual math skills.
  • 3-digit addition/subtraction with word problems.
  • Multiplication tables up to 12 × 12 and introduction to division.
  • Fractions: halves, quarters, thirds using pizza/circle visuals.
  • Place value till 10000 (Indian & international systems).
  • Measurements: length, weight, temperature (metric and customary units).
  • Basic geometry: points, lines, right angles, symmetry.
  • Data handling: bar graphs and pie charts.
  • Math puzzles & brain teasers (from global Olympiads).
🎯 Goal: Explore physical laws through observation, activity, and experiments.
  • Concept of force: push, pull, and friction (toy car experiments).
  • Motion and speed: fast, slow, distance, time.
  • Light and shadows: transparent, opaque, translucent (torch + paper).
  • Magnetism: poles, attraction/repulsion, magnetic materials.
  • States of matter (visual + temperature-based transitions).
🎯 Goal: Observe chemical behavior in real life and classify materials.
  • Soluble and insoluble substances (Chinese curriculum emphasis).
  • Evaporation and condensation (water cycle connection).
  • Physical vs. chemical change: vinegar + baking soda, ice melting.
  • Combustibility, magnetic nature, and hardness (property sorting).
  • Safe handling of household chemicals (soaps, vinegar, salt).
🎯 Goal: Understand systems in plants, humans, and animals with environment awareness.
  • Parts and functions of the digestive, respiratory, and skeletal systems (Indian + Chinese standard).
  • Seed germination and plant life cycles (U.S. Next Gen Science Standard aligned).
  • Food chains, ecosystems, and basic environmental care.
  • Animal classification (mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish).
  • Habitat-based comparison: desert, forest, water, tundra.
🎯 Goal: Strengthen comprehension, grammar, and early academic writing.
  • Reading level 2/3 storybooks with comprehension Q&A.
  • Grammar: tenses, punctuation, noun/adjective/verb types.
  • Essay writing: “My School,” “My Favorite Animal,” “Clean India.”
  • Creative writing using prompts and pictures.
  • Conversation practice and dialogues (ASL style).
  • Movie-based English summaries (as per Raising Nalanda policy).
🎯 Goal: Understand basic civic roles, community helpers, and responsibilities.
  • Who makes rules? – School, home, village, city, country.
  • Local government bodies (Panchayat, Nagar Nigam, comparison with Chinese “Residential Committees”).
  • Basic rights and duties (child-friendly version of Constitution).
  • Classroom democracy: student leader selection, voting.
  • Respecting diversity and religious/cultural festivals.
🎯 Goal: Understand money flow, local trade, and simple budgeting.
  • Types of occupations: farming, services, manufacturing.
  • Concept of bartering and simple trade simulation.
  • Saving, spending, and budgeting pocket money.
  • Roleplay: seller, buyer, bank officer, shopkeeper.
  • How price is decided: demand and supply basics.
🎯 Goal: Introduce timeline thinking and basic historical figures/events globally.
  • Family and local history timeline creation.
  • Life of great leaders: Gandhi, Nehru, Dr. Ambedkar, Mao Zedong, Confucius, Lincoln.
  • What is a civilization? (Mohenjo-Daro, Egyptian pyramids, Chinese dynasties).
  • Ancient transport and communication (Indian + Chinese inventions).
  • Classroom museum: bring old household item and describe.
🎯 Goal: Understand how land, water, and people shape the Earth.
  • Continents, oceans, equator, poles – using globe/map.
  • Mountains, rivers, plains, deserts: India + China + U.S.
  • Map reading: directions, legend, symbols.
  • Climate zones: tropical, temperate, polar (clothing/food comparison).
  • Natural disasters: flood, earthquake, storm (safety tips).
🎯 Goal: Build awareness of fairness, honesty, and lawful behavior.
  • Difference between rule and law (real-life examples).
  • Class court roleplay: “Who broke the rule?”
  • Fair vs. unfair treatment in games and school.
  • What is stealing, lying, bullying – and their effects.
  • Chinese system of “class duties” vs democratic classroom.
🎯 Goal: Develop Hindi reading, writing, and grammar for expression and culture.
  • Story reading from NCERT + class retelling.
  • Barakhadi fluency, matras, and basic sentence writing.
  • Vyakaran: sangya, visheshan, ling, kriya.
  • Poem recitation and small paragraph writing.
  • Write-your-own story with 5 lines and pictures.
🎯 Goal: Instill kindness, truthfulness, respect, and positive character.
  • Stories from Panchatantra, Confucian sayings, and real-life heroes.
  • Activity: “What would you do if…” situations (moral dilemmas).
  • Daily routine of respectful behavior at home and school.
  • Helping the weak, forgiving mistakes, showing patience.
  • End-of-month reflection on “Best Moral Act I Did”.
🎯 Goal: Foster multilingual understanding and communication skills.
  • English: Daily reading, speaking, and creative writing tasks.
  • Hindi: Grammar + vocabulary development through stories.
  • Third Language (optional):
    • Chinese: greetings, numbers, stroke order basics.
    • Sanskrit/Spanish: simple words, common phrases.
  • Activity: Translate 1 sentence into 2 languages daily.
🎯 Goal: Develop leadership, collaboration, and respect in group settings.
  • Group challenges: tower building, art collage, group puzzle.
  • Weekly Ambedkarian group discussions and project presentations.
  • Peer support activity: “Help your buddy” rotation task.
  • Score-sharing exams and team rotation (Nalanda model).
🎯 Goal: Boost strength, coordination, and team spirit through movement.
  • Running, hopping, skipping, yoga asanas.
  • Team sports: dodgeball, football basics, basketball dribbling.
  • Traditional Indian games: Kho-Kho, Langdi, Kabaddi.
  • Chinese coordination games (hand-eye, rhythm relay).
  • Daily 10-minute exercise/stretching session.
🎯 Goal: Broaden general awareness and curiosity about the world.
  • Important leaders, monuments, festivals of India, China, U.S.
  • Animal facts, planet names, fun inventions.
  • Current events: simplified news headlines explained weekly.
  • Monthly quiz competitions (team-based).
🎯 Goal: Stimulate exploration and questioning through small investigations.
  • How does a fan rotate? How do seeds grow? Simple experiments.
  • Using sensors in a toy (temperature, motion – as guided).
  • NPTEL content shown and explained in simplified form.
  • Mini presentations: each student explains one small discovery.
  • Group project: “Design a better lunchbox,” “Make a floating raft.”
