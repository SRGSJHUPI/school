  • How is FNI Calculated? The FNI score is calculated by evaluating multiple factors, including:
    • Annual Family Income (40 points): Lower incomes score closer to 1, higher incomes closer to 100.
    • Family Structure (20 points): Orphaned or single-parent families score lower.
    • Assets and Property (15 points): Ownership of land, house, or vehicles improves the score.
    • Financial Burden (10 points): Chronic medical conditions, debts, or loans reduce the score.
    • External Aid (5 points): Receiving social or government support adjusts the score.
    • Educational Expenses (10 points): Ability to afford books, uniforms, and supplies.
