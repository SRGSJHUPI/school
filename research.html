Research Projects (RP)

From local questions to global answers - Research Projects that matter.

What is Research Project?

Research project is a course at The Rising Nalanda. Research Projects are designed to immerse students in experimentation and hands-on innovation-encouraging them to explore how to think independently and apply their minds creatively. Starting from Class 6, they dive into electronics, mechanics, and software, gaining critical thinking, leadership, and research skills that prepare them for future innovation and global competitions.

Why Research Project?

Research Projects empower students to go beyond textbooks by engaging them in real-life problem-solving and innovation. For instance, when students design a Bluetooth-controlled RC car using Arduino, they not only apply coding and circuit design but also understand logical flow and troubleshooting-core skills in engineering. Similarly, making a drone or safety robot teaches them about aerodynamics, sensors, and mechanics in a way no theory class alone can. This hands-on exposure builds critical thinking, creativity, and teamwork while helping students realize where their true interests lie-whether in software, electronics, or mechanical systems. It also prepares them for competitions like IROC or Gujarat Robofest, boosting confidence and real-world competence. Ultimately, students learn to use their brains independently-to observe, experiment, and invent solutions, which is the true spirit of education.

Themes of Innovation

  • Safety Drone for school safety.
  • Solar-powered solutions for rural problems
  • Affordable health monitoring gadgets
  • Electricity generation from rainfall (Pelton turbine)
  • Waste-to-energy conversion techniques
  • Village infrastructure mapping using drones and so many others...

Technologies & Tools Used

  • Arduino and Raspberry Pi for physical computing
  • Basic electronics: sensors, relays, circuits
  • Fusion 360 and AutoCAD for design and prototyping
  • Python and C++ for automation and control logic

Mentorship and Evaluation

Projects are guided by dedicated RP mentors from the faculty and evaluated by a mixed panel of teachers and external engineers. Winners receive awards, certifications, and scholarships for further exploration.
